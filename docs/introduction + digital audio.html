digital audio

differences between digital and analog?
analog exists as is / digital can be manipulated (mastered)
continous (analog) / discreete (digital)
digital allows exact reproduction and analog is unique

8-track recorder (1966)

digital audio - personal computing

amiga commodore (1985) - first multimedia computer

digital audio - conceptually

- spatial arrangement
- different contexts / different publics
- reversible actions and experimentation
- exteremely low barrier to entry
cut/and paste, and sounds start to exist in space rather than just in time
to decide for whom it's played and what is your "ideal" audience
CTRL+Z opens up a space of possibilites
low barrier implies a multiplication of content and through quantity you can explore quality

digital audio workstations

digital audio workstation = hardware + software
the software is composed of a mixing/recording interface,
audio plug-ins,
vst instruments,
automation

audio plug-ins

they are additional pieces of software that integrate with the main application, essentially replacing their hardware equivalents
analyzing, creating and transforming
the two most popular types are VST and AU

analyzing

interprets the sound wave in a graphical way
time-frequency analysis shows the amplitude of all frequencies at a given moment

analyzing - 2

spectrogram shows the amplitude of each frequency at a given moment

creating

DAWs support sound synthesis, through virtual instruments
additive synthesis, substractive synthesis
notes are triggered by MIDI information

transforming

effects add character to the sound, and give it a particular ambience
spectral effects (EQ, Panning)
filters
time-based effects (reverb, delay, echo)
modulating effects (chorus, pitch-shift)
dynamic effects (compression, distortion)
envelopes give all of the above a different behaviour over time
spatial effects
low-pass / high-pass / band-pass
churches/big halls
envelopes are a timeline to what happens when
dry/wet signal is the ratio between unaffected signal and filtered signal

practical - reaper

importing audio files with best possible bit depth and sample rate
cleaning and processing it through noise removal
mixing multiple audio files (aka overdubbing)

exercise - soundscape

a soundscape is a non-traditional composition involving ambience (background), signals (foreground) and soundmarks (specific to the scene)
soundscapes are both objective (biosphere, geosphere, anthroposphere) and subjective (what matters most to the listener?)

exercise

using at least 4 different sound files, compose a soundscape that represents a particular place/moment
bbcsfx.acropolis.org.uk/ - bbc sound effects
freesound.org - creative commons sfx

recap

digital audio workstations allow for importing, editing and exporting audio files.
they also allow for spatial composition (through slicing, transporting and overdubbing along a timeline)
for experimentation (via the undo command, and by allowing to work on copies rather than on the direct source material)
and for greater cohesion through sound editing (effects and filters)
